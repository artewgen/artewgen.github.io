Overview of the Financial Health Project

The Financial Health (Personal Financial Management) project was designed to empower users with smarter ways to manage their finances. It combines actionable, personalised insights with intuitive tools to drive engagement within Tinkoff Bank’s mobile app.

Key highlights include:

  • Senior Designer Role: Led end-to-end design for Tinkoff AI Banking, from vision and strategy to launch and iteration.

  • User-Centric and Iterative Approaches: Refined the product through 15 deep-dive user research sessions and 60+ usability tests across 12 agile iterations, adapting it to user needs for a clear and frictionless experience.

This project highlights my ability to lead complex, high-impact work, drive business results, deepen user understanding, and advance fintech design.

Customer Needs: Financial Awareness

Despite Tinkoff’s feature-rich app, users couldn’t see their full financial picture. Switching between balances and charts, they still struggled to answer, “Am I really okay with money right now?”. This led to stress and missed engagement opportunities.

To address these challenges, I conducted 15 customer development sessions with the product manager focused on savings behaviours. It helped uncover and validate key user needs:

  • Understanding purchasing power: Users expressed a need to quickly assess their financial capacity for purchases, saying, “I want to know whether I can afford a large purchase.”
  • Planning future expenses: Many users highlighted the importance of tracking progress toward savings goals, such as vacations, stating, “I want to see how much I’ve saved and if I need to adjust my plan.”

🧭This feedback revealed a clear need for tools that simplify decision-making and help users confidently manage their finances.

Three Types of Customers

In collaboration with analysts, I examined the savings behaviour and financial intentions of 15 million Tinkoff customers, identifying three key segments:

  • 82% — the majority who spend their entire income without saving.
  • 12% — a modest segment who make an effort but save very little.
  • 6% — a small minority who save actively and have built capital.

🎯The primary focus was on active savers — a small segment, but one with real disposable income and strong financial potential.

Defining Success

FinHealth aimed to become the go-to space where users could gain confidence and find clear next steps—a completely new experience for Tinkoff.

For users, success was defined by understanding whether their financial situation was improving each month and identifying practical actions to make a positive impact

For the business, the goal was to drive feature adoption from a cold start, increase retention, and foster deep engagement that would lead to long-term loyalty and new revenue opportunities.

Dozen Design Iterations

I refined the design through about 12 iterations, testing prototypes with 60+ users, ranging from first-time savers to experienced investors.

The initial idea was to promote a slick “health score,” but users found it irrelevant and even discouraging. Moving away from scoring and focusing on real-life questions—like “Can I make it to payday?” and “What’s one thing I can do to improve?”—significantly increased user understanding and emotional relief. This method became central to FinHealth’s unique value proposition.

Quick interview tests were not standard practice at the company, so my manager and I established a new process that reduced testing time from weeks to days. Read about this improvement.

Financial Clarity: Tools, Tips, and Learning for Users

To give users a sense of control over their finances, I designed the Financial Parameters feature, offering clear monthly metrics.

Each parameter screen includes an overview, comparative charts, benchmarks, improvement tips, and related articles to support understanding and action.

Benchmarks translate financial status into practical terms.

“How long will the money last if income stops?”

“What percentage of income left?”

“How strong is your credit score for a loan?”

“What percentage of income is invested?”

Parameter statuses range from "Quite well" to "It’s perfect," using only positive language. Negative labels like “Low” are avoided. For edge cases, “Insufficient data” is shown with improvement tips instead of criticism.

Charts: Balancing Clarity and Detail

I explored various chart types, settling on wave and column charts. The challenge was presenting detailed information without cluttering the visual experience (easier said than done😅). I opted for a clean design, with additional information accessible through user interaction.

Personal Ideas: Financial Growth and Success

The Personal Ideas is a key feature that, after benchmarking and understanding users’ financial situations, immediately delivers tailored, practical suggestions. It helps users improve their finances with actionable advice and supports business goals like cross-selling by recommending relevant products and services.

Over 40 actionable suggestions were developed in collaboration with the marketing team.

Articles: Enhancing Financial Literacy

Tinkoff’s Finance Journal reaches over 20 million readers each month. Integrating these articles into the metrics screen enhances client engagement and promotes financial literacy within the app.

Conclusions: Delivering Financial Clarity

This cohesive approach combines clear metrics, comparative benchmarks, and actionable advice to give users real financial clarity.

By integrating tools, tips, and learning resources, it empowers users to understand their finances better, make informed decisions, and build lasting financial habits—ultimately driving higher engagement and retention for the business.

MVP and Testing

Before launching the full version, we released an MVP without ratings, credit scores, or articles to quickly test core metrics and gather feedback. This surfaced bugs, like miscategorised transfers, and usability issues such as the need for long-tap on charts—highlighting the value of thorough internal testing.

🏆The MVP achieved a notable 40% retention rate, an impressive result for a minimal version.

Final launch and Outstanding Outcomes

We launched the final version of FinHealth after a successful beta, and it delivered beyond expectations 🎉 — a third of initial users became regulars after launch, retention in the app increased, and we saw user feedback move from transactional “just works” to emotional “this finally makes sense”. FinHealth delivered:

  • 32% retention rate, driving loyalty and supporting adaptation for clients.
  • 1.4 percentage point boost in 30-day app retention, adding ~$7M in monthly revenue without acquisition costs.
  • Strong cross-selling, with 10% of users opening new financial products via personalised recommendations.
  • Higher financial literacy, as 30% engaged with educational articles.
  • Replicated success, inspiring a B2B Financial Health feature based on these results.

These outcomes highlight FinHealth's role as a transformative feature within Tinkoff’s ecosystem, delivering measurable benefits to users and the business.

Future plans

Together with the product manager, we planned the next evolution of FinHealth to deepen user value and engagement. Upcoming features include:

  • Subscription manager: a top-requested feature, designed to help users track, manage, and cancel subscriptions with ease. Now in development.
  • Capital editing: enables users to see their true financial picture by adding assets from other banks, real estate, and external savings—ensuring “total wealth” reflects their complete portfolio.
  • Smart savings goals: smart planning tools to help users set, adjust, and achieve financial goals with proactive recommendations.
  • Social profiles: a feature to make it easier to interact with social finance, fostering community and user support.

Key Takeaways from FinHealth

My biggest takeaway is that users want context and support, not just data, and are eager to build new habits when they feel encouraged.

Working on FinHealth inside a mature app showed me that real innovation means championing bold ideas and creating emotional value, not just functional features. FinHealth isn’t just a new dashboard—it’s proof of what’s possible when a team builds for trust and clarity, enabling true financial confidence.
