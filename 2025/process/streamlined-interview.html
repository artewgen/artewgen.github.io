Guidelines for Effective User Interviews

To support effective interviews, I developed detailed guidelines and a script based on my interview experience, highlighting key principles:

  • Define clear objectives for each session: E.g., focus on testing a new feature or validating assumptions about user behaviour.
  • Recruit participants matching your target users: If building product for investors, prioritise actual investors over internal staff.
  • Begin with introductions and warm-up questions: Start with, ‚ÄúCan you describe how you use finance apps?‚Äù to help participants feel comfortable.
  • Record only with consent and clarify confidentiality: Say, ‚ÄúMay I record this session? It‚Äôs confidential and for internal use only.‚Äù
  • Use open-ended, neutral questions: Ask, ‚ÄúWhat did you think about onboarding?‚Äù not ‚ÄúWas onboarding easy for you?‚Äù
  • Pause and use follow-up questions for depth: After an answer, ask, ‚ÄúCan you tell me why you chose that?‚Äù or ‚ÄúWhat did you expect to happen next?‚Äù
  • Summarise to confirm understanding: Reflect, ‚ÄúSo, you were unsure how to set a savings goal in the app, is that right?‚Äù
  • Encourage final comments and clarify next steps: End with, ‚ÄúAnything else you‚Äôd like to add?‚Äù and a brief explanation of how their feedback will be used.

Insights for Mobile Testing

Initially, I tested prototypes by sharing my desktop and asking users to describe their actions. Later, I sent links for users to open prototypes on their own phones and share screens via Zoom or Google Meet. This led to more realistic feedback and allowed testing of real interactions like taps and swipes.

Benefits:

  • More natural user behaviour;
  • Ability to test gestures and animations.

Challenges:

  • Some respondents struggled to join from their phone, open prototypes, or share screens;
  • Figma browser bars reduced realism of native app;
  • Sometimes prototypes displayed inconsistently or incorrectly across user devices.

Afterwards, I explored the Figma Mirror app for seamless full-screen testing, which resolved most prototype display issues, although it does require respondents to install the app in advance.
