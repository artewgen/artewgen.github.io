Merchant Problems: Mobile Access Limitations

YooMoney initially offered only a web-based merchant account, with no adaptive or mobile version available. Merchants frequently voiced frustration at being unable to access key features on the go, but simply adapting the website was technically and functionally unfeasible. This led to creating native mobile apps for iOS, Android, and iPad.

I conducted about 20 user interviews, where merchants highlighted their most significant needs for mobile:

  • Instant access to payment and sales analytics.
  • Full control over payments with advanced search and filtering.
  • Fast, seamless creation of invoices and processing of refunds.
  • Direct access to customer support.

üß≠This research shaped a roadmap, ensuring the mobile apps delivered meaningful value rather than just a scaled-down web experience.

Adaptation: Rethinking Business Tools for Mobile

When transitioning the established YooMoney web service to mobile, the challenge was not to recreate desktop workflows but to thoughtfully reimagine core business management functionalities for mobile devices' constraints and unique possibilities.

Instead of simply copying web layouts, I reimagined the information architecture and prioritised high-frequency actions like analytics, payments, and invoicing. This meant simplifying navigation, designing gesture-based interactions, and optimising onboarding for speed.

Analytics in Focus

In the initial release, the main screen displayed a list of payments, with analytics on the second tab. From early user reviews, I learned that merchants were accessing analytics more frequently than the payments list. In response, analytics was moved to the primary position in the next update, and users welcomed the change.

The analytics dashboard featured nine key metrics: revenue, average check size, refund amount and count, number of payments, paid bills, average bill receipt, invoices issued, and unpaid bills.

Users especially appreciated the ability to compare data via an interactive chart that could be expanded in horizontal mode‚Äîa feature that received consistently high ratings and positive feedback.

üìà Many merchants shared that they opened the app first thing in the morning to check analytics, treating it as their business dashboard. After moving analytics to the main screen, daily active sessions increased by 35%.

Fast Payment Actions

The payments section provided merchants with a comprehensive, searchable list of all transactions, putting essential information at their fingertips. Merchants could confirm or cancel payments, review detailed histories, and search for specific transactions easily.

Users could see a complete timeline for each payment: payment status, receipt issuance, and any associated refunds. The ability to quickly view and send receipts proved especially valuable, becoming one of merchants' most requested and well-used features.

üéØ A lot of users highlighted frustration with web-based workflows that buried transaction details several clicks deep. Surfacing this data on one screen and enabling actions with minimal taps was a major usability win on mobile.

Instant Invoicing and Refunds

The app made invoicing fast and effortless‚Äîmerchants could generate and send invoices instantly via email, SMS, or a direct link, allowing buyers to pay on the spot from any device. Once a payment was received, the merchant was notified with a push notification, keeping cash flow up to date in real time.

Merchants also had the flexibility to issue full or partial refunds and could set expiration dates for invoices, preventing payments after a specified deadline.

Real-Time Merchant Support

After hearing the need during user interviews, I added real-time support chat to the app. This allowed merchants to get help without leaving their workflow and made support faster and more effective by letting them send messages, screenshots, and transaction details directly to the support team.

üí¨ By embedding chat within the mobile app, average support response times were reduced by 35%, and merchant satisfaction scores increased significantly.
