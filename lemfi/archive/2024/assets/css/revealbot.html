Revealbot is powerful automation platform designed to help businesses efficiently manage and scale their digital ad campaigns across various platforms like Instagram, TikTok, Meta and Snapchat.

Enhancing signup process

I'm working at Revealbot several months. My journey here started with a memorable job interview experience, which led to my first impactful project: the redesign of the company’s signup process.

Instead of repeating my design cases at the interview I focused on some ideas I had for improving the company’s signup process. I conducted in-depth research and prepared a detailed case study on enhancing signup flow, using a comprehensive Customer Journey Map (CJM).

A little preview: by redesigning the signup form my team and I managed:

  • speed up the user registration process by 3x;
  • increase conversion for about 5%.

User persona

I studied the marketing domain and Revealbot, and defined a persona Emily through which I planned to evaluate the registration process.

I defined Emily's pain points:

  • challenges with time limits;
  • struggles with analyzing big data for correct advertising choices;
  • looking for efficient and intuitive solutions to enhance marketing campaigns;
  • wants to start exploring a new tool quickly and easily.

Then, after going through all the steps of the signup process, I gathered the feedback and issues I discovered.

CJM

When creating the CJM, I was inspired by Growth.Design's approach, where they showcase the current flow and then propose improvements by presenting the new one.

In case I showed Emily’s mood bar and illustrated how it shifts throughout the signup process, highlighting not only problem areas, but the positive aspects too.

Every problem point I described with data insights and UX statements. For instance, according to a statistic I found on PR Newswire, 67% of people who abandon lengthy online forms never return. So, it’s crucial to request only the essential information.

The major problems

1. Too much data required

The signup process asked the user to fill in too much information.

  • steps that can be skipped or simplified;
  • a lot of unnecessary data required that isn’t used;
  • too many options to choose from in dropdowns.

2. Complex email confirmation

A complicated confirmation process that’s easy to get stuck on and prevents further registration process.

  • dead-end at the start of confirmation;
  • requires finding the right email;
  • after confirmation, signup doesn’t continue but redirects to the main screen.

3. Disruptive popups

During the registration process, numerous modals and popups appear, asking for additional information and making it difficult or impossible to close.

Ka-boom

In the end, Emily was unable to complete the signup process and abandoned it.

I presented the entire CJM showing how Emily was going the registration process.

New flow

I proposed a new, more seamless and quicker signup flow, without requesting unnecessary information, featuring quick social media signup, fast and convenient email confirmation, along with other improvements.

Social media auth

I suggested fast signup via Google and Facebook. I discovered that Formstack tripled their conversion rates by adding a social media autofill feature.

Email provider detection

Detecting the user’s email provider by their email and offering a direct link to the email service made the process faster and easier, without any disruptions.

It works for Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo, Proton and iCloud.

Sniper links

Sniper links help display emails from the required domain by adding necessary parameters to the URL. I found out that it helped Growth.Design's to boost by 7% their confirmation rates.

It also works for Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo, Proton and iCloud.

Faster process

I suggested reducing additional steps and making the process as short and seamless as possible, with any extra information being requested from users after registration and the “aha” moment.

The idea became a reality

The team loved my proposal—it was a hit. As it turned out, Revealbot’s team was already working on that exact issue, and my timing couldn’t have been better. Curiously enough, those ideas later became the basis of my onboarding task, and I had the opportunity to implement them in production.

This is how the updated signup looks in production now.

Small changes

Certainly, the concept and the final developed product can vary. My concept was no exception and experienced some changes, which I’ll share.

- Facebook auth. We decided not to implement Facebook auth because significant of our users are agencies, and their Facebook accounts differ from the ones they manage for their clients. For now, we’ve implemented quick login only through Google.

- Not so short. We couldn’t create a very short and quick version because it requires adding information requests within the product itself, which was decided to be done during the onboarding update.

- Mobile version. I discovered that over 60% of users access the registration from a mobile device, so we added a responsive version for the signup process to make it more convenient for mobile users.

Futute plans

  • reducing additional steps and making the process shorter as I concepted;
  • improving the next flow after signup — onboarding flow;

Final results

I’m excited to share the results our team accomplished after several months of hard work — we tripled the speed of the user registration process and boosted conversion rates by approximately 5%.
