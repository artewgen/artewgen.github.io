Insights for Mobile Testing

Initially, I tested prototypes by sharing my desktop and asking users to describe their actions. Later, I sent links for users to open prototypes on their own phones and share screens via Zoom or Google Meet. This led to more realistic feedback and allowed testing of real interactions like taps and swipes.

Benefits:

  • More natural user behaviour;
  • Ability to test gestures and animations.

Challenges:

  • Some respondents struggled to join from their phone, open prototypes, or share screens;
  • Figma browser bars reduced realism of native app;
  • Sometimes prototypes displayed inconsistently or incorrectly across user devices.

Afterwards, I explored the Figma Mirror app for seamless full-screen testing, which resolved most prototype display issues, although it does require respondents to install the app in advance.
